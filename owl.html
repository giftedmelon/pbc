GREAT HORNED OWL

NAME

Great horned owl

HABITAT

Lage part of North America

CLASSIFICATION SPECIES

Bird

SIZE

60 cm, 200 cm (wingspan), 1.5 kg

ECOLOGY

They usually eat rodents, frogs, scorpions and other smaller birds. But they sometimes prey on bigger animals like dogs. Their favourite may be skunks. They live in nearly all environments like in deserts, wetlands, forests, grasslands, backyards and cities. They sometimes swallow their prey whole and later they spit off parts like bone and fur.

CULTURAL REPRESENTATION

They are regarded by many tribes of Native Americans as a symbol of strength, courage and beauty. There are ceremonies and myths concerning them. For example, The Pima of the Southwest believed that owls were reincarnations of slain warriors who fly about by night.
