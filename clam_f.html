CLAM

NAME

Lajonkairia lajonkairii (Common names include Manila clam, Japanese littleneck clam, Japanese cockle, and Japanese carpet shell)

HABITAT

Originally inhabit Indian, Philippines and Pacific Oceans from Pakistan and India north to China, Japan, Korea and the Kuril Islands. Now live along the coast of Europe, America and Middle Eastern countries.

CLASSIFICATION SPECIES

Bivalve

SIZE

5cm

ECOLOGY

It lives under the level of 10 cm depth. It is a saltwater species and feeds phytoplankton by filtering seawater. Mature clams breed up to 2.4 million eggs. The larva starts to synthesize a shell two days after hatching. It lives up to 14 years.

CULTURAL REPRESENTATION

The clam is frequently eaten in Asia, especially in the Philippines, Korea, and Japan. Dishes using the clam are, for example, Japanese Udon and Korean kongnamul-guk. Manila clam represents 25% of commercially produced molluscs in the world. It was once bred in Nohara lab, famous for being a shelter for a lot of plants and animals, especially moulds and bacteria.
